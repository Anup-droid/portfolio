My Areas of Interest

My work sits at the intersection of statistical methodology and Health Technology Assessment — from structuring the evidence base through to informing payer submissions and reimbursement decisions.

Systematic Literature Reviews

Designing and executing SLRs to identify, screen, and extract evidence from clinical trials and observational studies — the evidence base underpinning all downstream ITC and HTA analyses.

Indirect Treatment Comparisons (ITC)

Leading ITC projects — from protocol development and feasibility assessment to final analysis — generating comparative evidence where head-to-head trials are absent, for global HTA submissions and payer negotiations.

Network Meta-Analysis (NMA)

Building frequentist and Bayesian NMA models to simultaneously rank multiple treatments using both direct and indirect evidence, with full heterogeneity and inconsistency diagnostics.

Population-Adjusted Comparisons

Applying MAIC, STC (G-computation), and ML-NMR to bridge between trial populations and HTA decision target populations, using individual patient data (IPD) to align with aggregate comparator data.

Survival Analysis & Extrapolation

Fitting parametric and flexible survival models to immature trial data for long-term extrapolation, incorporating external registry or RWE sources using Bayesian informative priors — a key input to cost-effectiveness models in oncology and rare disease HTA.

Meta-regression & ML-NMR

Applying meta-regression and Multilevel Network Meta-Regression (ML-NMR) in R to resolve heterogeneity across trials, account for patient-level effect modifiers, and produce both conditional and marginal treatment estimates in any target population.
